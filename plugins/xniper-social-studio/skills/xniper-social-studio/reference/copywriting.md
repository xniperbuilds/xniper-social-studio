# Copywriting — Hooks, Headlines, CTAs

Design carries the eye; copy lands the message. Keep it tight. The machine-
readable hook library is in `data/hooks.json`; this is the how-to.

---

## Hooks (slide 1 / the thumb-stopper)

The first line decides whether anyone reads the rest. Formulas:

- **Question hook** — "Still designing posts by hand?"
- **Bold claim** — "Your captions are killing your reach."
- **Number/list** — "7 layout mistakes that scream amateur."
- **Callout** — "For founders who hate Canva."
- **Curiosity gap** — "Nobody tells you this about carousels."
- **Contrarian** — "Stop posting daily. Do this instead."
- **Before/after** — "From 200 to 20k views in 30 days."
- **Mistake** — "The #1 reason your posts flop."

Rules: ≤ ~8 words, concrete, no hashtags on the hook itself, no brand name on
slide 1 (the hook earns attention first).

## Headlines

- One idea. ≤ 8 words. Front-load the strongest word.
- Specific beats vague: "Beat 50 levels in one tap" > "A fun new game".
- Use the accent color or italic on the single most important word.
- Cut filler: "Introducing the all-new…" → just say what it is.

## Subheads / body

- One supporting line. Max ~20 words. Answers "so what?"
- Plain and functional beats clever-but-confusing.

## CTAs

- One per graphic, action verb first: *Play now · Get the free guide · Save this
  · Follow for more · Link in bio · Shop the drop · Read the thread.*
- ≤ 3 words on a button. Make it visually unmistakable (accent fill).
- Don't stack synonyms ("Get started" + "Sign up" + "Try free") — pick one.

## Voice tips

- Match the platform: LinkedIn = sharper/pro; IG/TikTok = punchy/casual;
  Pinterest = how-to/benefit-led.
- Read every visible string back before shipping. Cut anything broken, cringe,
  or fake-precise. Boring-but-clear > clever-but-wrong.
